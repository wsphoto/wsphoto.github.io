Long Exposure - Neon Grooves

As a lover of music, I collect records in my spare time. I love the look, feel, and sense of ownership that come along with vinyl records.
You can usually find me playing a record and relaxing to it in my room at the end of every day. That's where my idea for this photo sparked.
While I was listening to a record I thought - wouldn't it be cool if I could capture the motion of a record spinning with light? The result is this piece, called Neon Grooves.
I managed to capture the same grooves and texture of a record, while capturing the spinnning motion of a disc on a turntable.


Double Exposure - Star Spangled

As I watched a flag wave in the wind, I contemplated what this symbol means to so many.
I wanted this image to convey the American mantra of “E Pluribus Unum,” or, “out of many, one.” In order to do this the best, I chose to make the image a double exposure.
In our divided nation, this has been an important thing to remember--we are all united under the same flag regardless of our race, creed, or background.
By showing the flag in multiple stages in one piece it represents that it takes all of us, regardless of our difference, to make a whole nation.


Low Key - In Control

In my free time outside of photography, I enjoy occassionally DJ-ing for fun!
This picture was an attempt to capture the intricacies of a DJ controller that might go unnoticed by someone unfamiliar with the craft.
I chose to make the image low key because I felt that it added a sense of focus to the piece that it did not have in color.


Bokeh - Libation

In this image, I attempted to capture a feeling of emptiness.
The use of bokeh in the image allowed me to contrast the warmth and fullness of light with the void of the glass.
In our lives, we often find ourselves chaasing after what we deem will make us happy, when in reality, it will not.
Instead of being thankful for the things we have, we can often lose sight of what really matters in life, and find ourselves with an empty glass.
This image serves as a reminder to stop and be thankful.
